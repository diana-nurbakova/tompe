## What is this study about?

You are using the **ToM-PE** platform as part of your coursework in machine translation post-editing. This platform is also part of an ongoing research project investigating how translation students develop error detection and post-editing skills, and how different types of scaffolding and feedback support that development.

## What data is collected?

As you use the platform, the following data is automatically recorded:

- **Annotation decisions**: which errors you identify, how you classify them, and the severity you assign
- **Justification texts**: the reasoning you write to explain your annotation decisions
- **Post-editing actions**: the changes you make when editing translations
- **Performance metrics**: your accuracy scores, detection rates, and progression over time
- **Timing data**: how long you spend on each item and exercise
- **Session metadata**: which exercises you completed and when

Your real name is **never** included in research data. All data is linked to a pseudonymous identifier (student ID).

## How will the data be used?

We are asking for your consent on two separate levels. You may accept or decline each independently.

### Tier 1 — Research analysis of interaction data

Your interaction data (annotations, scores, timing, progression patterns) may be aggregated and analyzed as part of published research on MT post-editing pedagogy. Individual results are always reported anonymously or as group averages.

### Tier 2 — Anonymized text excerpts in publications

Short excerpts from your justification texts may be quoted in academic publications as examples of student reasoning. All excerpts are fully anonymized — no names, student IDs, or identifying details are included.

## Important: your rights

- **Voluntary participation**: Consent is entirely voluntary. Declining does **not** affect your grade, your access to the platform, or the feedback you receive. You can use ToM-PE for your coursework regardless of your consent decision.
- **Right to withdraw**: You may withdraw your consent at any time through the platform. If you withdraw, your data will be excluded from all future research analyses. Data already published in anonymized, aggregated form cannot be retroactively removed.
- **Data protection**: Your data is stored securely and processed in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Only the research team has access to the pseudonymized data.
- **Contact**: If you have questions about this study or your data, contact [instructor email]. To raise concerns about research ethics, contact [ethics committee email].
