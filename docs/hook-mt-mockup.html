It reads perfectly — and it says the opposite.

eventually English
in the end; sooner or later. The agreement will take effect.
≠
éventuellement Français
possibly; perhaps; if needed. The agreement might take effect.

A false cognate: identical-looking words, opposite meanings. The fluent French quietly weakened a certainty into a maybe. The correct word was finalement. We read it, it looked right, and we approved it. That is the fluency trap.
